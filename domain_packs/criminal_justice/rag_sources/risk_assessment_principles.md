# Principles for the Use of Algorithmic Risk Assessment

These principles describe how risk-assessment tools should be used to support parole and supervision
decisions. They are illustrative reference material for the SENTINEL assistant.

## Advisory, never determinative
A risk score is advisory information for a human decision-maker. It must never be the sole basis for
a decision to detain, release, or set supervision conditions. A trained officer makes the final
decision and may override the score with a documented reason.

## Calibration and uncertainty
Scores must be calibrated: among people assigned a 30% risk, roughly 30% should reoffend. Every score
is accompanied by an uncertainty band; officers should treat scores near a band boundary with
particular caution and weight individual circumstances more heavily.

## Explainability
Each prediction is accompanied by the factors that most influenced it. Static factors such as age and
criminal history cannot be changed; dynamic factors such as employment and substance use can, and
should guide supervision and support rather than punishment.

## Proportionality
Supervision intensity should be proportional to assessed risk and need. Over-supervision of low-risk
individuals is both costly and criminogenic; resources should concentrate on higher-risk cases.
